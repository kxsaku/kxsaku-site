Resources · Terms · Policies

Clear expectations. Clean work. No surprises.

This page covers how SNS works with clients: payments, subscriptions, cancellations, access, data handling, and basic service boundaries. It’s written to be readable, not buried in fluff.

Quick Summary
  • Work begins after scope + approval (and deposit if required).
  • Subscriptions are billed on schedule; non-payment can pause service.
  • Client-owned accounts stay client-owned. Access is permission-based.
  • Sensitive info is minimized; only what’s needed to do the job.

Client Terms & Policies

Last updated:

1) Scope of Work
What’s included, what isn’t, and how changes are handled.
⌄
Work is based on the agreed scope (what’s being installed/configured, where, and why). If the job changes midstream (new rooms, new equipment, new requirements), I’ll confirm the change before continuing so time + cost stay predictable.
2) Estimates, Deposits, and Scheduling
How booking works for on-site visits and projects.
⌄
Estimates are based on the information available at the time. Some issues only become visible on-site (cabling quality, interference, ISP constraints, legacy config, unmanaged switches, etc.). For certain projects, a deposit may be required to reserve time and/or procure equipment.
3) Payments (One-Time Work)
Invoices, due dates, and what happens if payment is late.
⌄
One-time work is invoiced after completion unless otherwise agreed in writing. If an invoice becomes overdue, support may pause until the balance is resolved. If hardware was procured on your behalf, it remains payable regardless of deployment status.
4) Subscription Support (Membership)
Ongoing support rules, billing, and termination for non-payment.
⌄
Subscription support is billed on the schedule shown at checkout. If a subscription payment fails or becomes past due, service can be paused until the subscription is brought current. Repeated non-payment may result in cancellation and removal of ongoing access (admin accounts, dashboards, monitoring links, etc.).
5) Access, Admin Accounts, and Ownership
Who owns what, and how credentials are handled.
⌄
Client-owned systems remain client-owned (ISP account, router cloud account, domain/DNS, Wi-Fi controller, etc.). If SNS is granted admin access, it’s for performing the agreed work only. When requested, access can be reduced or removed after the job is complete.
6) Data Handling and Privacy
What information is collected and how it’s used.
⌄
SNS minimizes data collection. Personal details submitted through the site (name, email, phone, service address) are used only to respond, schedule, and deliver service. Device details (models, configs, screenshots/logs) are used for troubleshooting and may be retained to support continuity—especially for subscription clients.
7) Third-Party Services
ISP, cloud portals, Stripe, vendors, and platform dependencies.
⌄
Some services depend on third parties (ISP uptime, vendor cloud portals, authentication providers, payment processors). SNS can configure and harden, but cannot guarantee third-party availability. If a third party changes terms, pricing, or features, SNS will adapt the implementation where reasonable.
8) Acceptable Use (Client Portal + Website)
Rules for using the site, inquiry forms, and client access.
⌄
Do not submit false information, impersonate others, attempt unauthorized access, or abuse automated systems. SNS may block access to protect systems, clients, and service quality.
9) Service Limitations
What SNS can and cannot guarantee in real networks.
⌄
Networking work involves variables outside any one party’s control (building materials, RF congestion, aging cabling, ISP limitations, vendor firmware behavior). SNS focuses on measurable improvement and stability, but does not guarantee “perfect” Wi-Fi everywhere without addressing the real constraints discovered during assessment.
10) Changes to These Terms
How updates are communicated.
⌄
SNS may update these terms to reflect product changes, policy improvements, or legal requirements. The “Last updated” date above reflects the most recent revision.
